LO 1
ON EXAM — ALL steroids share cyclopentanoperhydrophenanthrene 4-ring nucleus. Cholesterol = C27. Classes: C21 PREGNANES, C19 ANDROSTANES, C18 ESTRANES. α = below plane, β = above. Cortisol = 11β-OH, 17α-OH, 21-OH, 3-oxo-Δ4. Aldosterone = distinctive 18-aldehyde
Cholesterol backbone (LO 1)
Cyclopentanoperhydrophenanthrene — 17-carbon sterol nucleus shared by all steroids
Three 6-member rings (A, B, C) + one 5-member ring (D). 17 ring carbons + angular methyls at C-18, C-19 + side chain at C-17
A (upper left), B (lower left), C (lower right), D (upper right — the 5-member ring)
27 carbons total (17 in nucleus + 8 in side chain + 2 methyls at C-18/C-19)
5-member ring containing C-13, C-14, C-15, C-16, C-17; carries the side chain at C-17 and is where androgens/estrogens differ from pregnanes
Starts at A-ring top-left, runs clockwise around outer ring, then inward. C-18 = methyl on C-13 (between C/D rings); C-19 = methyl on C-10 (between A/B). Side chain: C-20 to C-27

Nomenclature conventions (LO 1)
Ketone (C=O) at the numbered carbon. Cortisol has 3-one and 20-one; aldosterone has 3-one, 20-one, and 18-one (aldehyde)
Hydroxyl (-OH) at the numbered carbon. Cortisol: 11β-ol, 17α-ol, 21-ol. Estradiol: 3-ol (phenolic), 17β-ol
Δn or "-ene" = double bond, numbered from lower-numbered carbon. Cortisol is Δ4 (between C-4/C-5). Δ5 steroids (pregnenolone, DHEA) have double bond C5-C6
α = group below plane (dashed line). β = above plane (solid wedge). Angular methyls at C-18, C-19 are β — so β = same side as them
Aromatase (CYP19) removes C-19 methyl + introduces three A-ring double bonds → phenolic aromatic A-ring. Converts C19 androgens → C18 estrogens
Named because it adds a hydroxyl at C-11 with β-orientation (above plane)

After pregnenolone (LO 2)
Pregnenolone → progesterone; 17-OH-pregnenolone → 17-OH-progesterone; DHEA → androstenedione. Converts Δ5 to Δ4
Smooth ER (some mitochondrial isoforms)
Δ5 steroids (pregnenolone, 17-OH-pregnenolone, DHEA) have C5-C6 double bond + 3β-OH. 3β-HSD isomerizes to C4-C5 and oxidizes 3β-OH to 3-oxo → Δ4 forms. Required for active hormones
Made in mitochondria by CYP11A1, pregnenolone exits to cytosol, enters smooth ER for further modification (CYP17, 3β-HSD, CYP21), then SHUTTLES BACK to mitochondria for CYP11B1/B2 in the final steps of cortisol and aldosterone synthesis
Shuttle logic (LO 2)
CYP11A1 (cholesterol → pregnenolone), CYP11B1 (11-deoxycortisol → cortisol; DOC → corticosterone), CYP11B2 (corticosterone → aldosterone)
CYP17 (17α-hydroxylation + 17,20-lyase), CYP21A2 (21-hydroxylation), 3β-HSD, 17β-HSD, aromatase
Steroid intermediates shuttle between mitochondria and SER multiple times. Pregnenolone: mito → SER (17-OH or 3β-HSD) → SER (21-OH) → back to mito for 11β-OH → cortisol. Aldosterone: pathway ends with final 3 reactions all in mitochondria by CYP11B2
LO 3

Glomerulosa LACKS CYP17 → can't make cortisol or androgens. Aldosterone synthase (CYP11B2) ONLY in glomerulosa

Why regulation differs by zone (LO 3)
AT1 receptor on glomerulosa (Gq-coupled → IP3 → ↑Ca2+ → aldo synthesis and release; chronic stim ↑ CYP11B2 transcription)

11β-HSD — cortisol/cortisone switch (LO 5)
Expressed in aldosterone target tissues — kidney collecting duct + distal colon + salivary glands + placenta. Converts cortisol (active at MR) → cortisone (INACTIVE). Prevents cortisol from activating MR
MR has SIMILAR affinity for cortisol and aldosterone. Cortisol plasma ~100-1000× higher than aldo → MR would be saturated by cortisol. 11β-HSD2 in MR target cells converts cortisol → cortisone → MR selectively activated by aldosterone
Expressed in liver + adipose + CNS (glucocorticoid target tissues). Converts cortisone → cortisol (REGENERATES active hormone). Why oral cortisone works (after hepatic activation). Inhibitors studied for metabolic syndrome
Glycyrrhetinic acid INHIBITS 11β-HSD2 → cortisol accumulates in kidney → activates MR → APPARENT MINERALOCORTICOID EXCESS (HTN + hypokalemia + metabolic alkalosis; aldo AND renin LOW — RAAS suppressed)
AME (Apparent Mineralocorticoid Excess) syndrome — autosomal recessive. Childhood severe HTN + hypokalemia + metabolic alkalosis + low renin + low aldo + normal cortisol. Treat with dexamethasone or spironolactone
Very high cortisol OVERWHELMS kidney 11β-HSD2 → cortisol binds MR → HTN + hypokalemia (especially ectopic ACTH). Mineralocorticoid-like picture without true aldo excess
Carrier protein changes (LO 5)
Estrogen drives hepatic CBG synthesis → CBG ↑ → total cortisol ↑. Free cortisol and ACTH remain normal → eucorticotropic
Liver failure → ↓ CBG synthesis. Nephrotic → urinary CBG loss. Total cortisol ↓. Free cortisol may remain normal
Free cortisol is active fraction; unchanged when CBG varies (free-hormone hypothesis). Short-term, free cortisol defended by HPA axis; total reflects carrier-hormone pool
Free cortisol — 24-h urine free cortisol (UFC), late-night salivary cortisol, or direct plasma free cortisol. Preferred when CBG abnormal (pregnancy, OCPs, liver disease)
LO 6
ON EXAM — ALDO on MR → ENaC, Na-K-ATPase, ROMK, SGK1 → Na+/H2O retention + K+ and H+ secretion (HTN + hypokalemia + metabolic alkalosis). CORTISOL on GR (widely expressed): trans-activation (PEPCK, G6Pase, annexin-1, IκB) + trans-repression (blocks NF-κB, AP-1 → anti-inflammatory). 11β-HSD2 protects MR from cortisol. Spironolactone/eplerenone/finerenone = MR antagonists

Tissue-specific cortisol effects (LO 6)
↑ gluconeogenic enzymes (PEPCK, G6Pase, F-1,6-BPase) → ↑ hepatic glucose output. Also ↑ glycogen synthesis (preserves glycogen). Overall: ↑ blood glucose (diabetogenic)
PROTEOLYSIS — stimulates ubiquitin-proteasome degradation of myofibrils; reduces AA uptake. Net: muscle wasting + proximal weakness (classic Cushing); AAs released to liver for gluconeogenesis
REGIONAL DIFFERENTIAL — lipolysis in subcutaneous limb fat (thin arms/legs in Cushing); lipogenesis in CENTRAL/TRUNCAL depots (moon facies, buffalo hump). Mechanism: different 11β-HSD1 + insulin sensitivity across depots
IMMUNOSUPPRESSION — lymphocyte apoptosis (↓ lymphocytes); inhibits cytokines (NF-κB block); inhibits PLA2 via annexin-1 (↓ PGs/LTs); demargination of neutrophils (↑ neutrophil count). Risk of opportunistic infection
Osteoporosis — direct inhibition of osteoblasts + stimulation of osteoclasts + ↑ RANKL. Also ↓ intestinal Ca2+ absorption + ↑ urinary Ca2+ → secondary hyperparathyroidism. Hallmark Cushing complication
PERMISSIVE for catecholamines — cortisol up-regulates α1-adrenergic receptors and increases vascular smooth muscle sensitivity to NE/Epi → maintains vascular tone + BP. Why Addisonian crisis includes hypotension refractory to pressors without cortisol
MR specificity — 11β-HSD2 trick (LO 6)
Similar or slightly higher for cortisol than aldosterone. MR is "mineralocorticoid" by physiologic function, not intrinsic affinity
Plasma cortisol ~100-1000× higher than aldosterone
Cortisol would saturate MR in kidney → chronic Na+ retention + K+ loss + HTN → permanent "apparent mineralocorticoid excess"
11β-HSD2 in MR target cells oxidizes cortisol's 11β-OH → 11-ketone → cortisone (low MR affinity). "Pre-receptor metabolism" makes MR functionally specific for aldosterone
(1) Licorice (glycyrrhetinic acid) inhibits → AME. (2) Congenital 11β-HSD2 mutation → AME in childhood. (3) Cushing — very high cortisol overwhelms capacity → cortisol spills onto MR → HTN + hypokalemia
Pharmacology of MR and GR (LO 6)
MR antagonist (competitive). Used for: Conn, heart failure (RALES), resistant HTN, Cushing, ascites. Side effects: gynecomastia + impotence (AR antagonism), menstrual irregularities (PR activity), hyperkalemia
Selective MR antagonist — minimal AR/PR activity. Fewer sex-hormone side effects. Used in heart failure (EPHESUS post-MI), primary aldo, resistant HTN. Hyperkalemia still a risk
Nonsteroidal MR antagonist. Approved for DIABETIC CKD — reduces CV events + CKD progression. Less hyperkalemia than steroidal MR antagonists
Dexamethasone = pure GR agonist (negligible MR activity) — preferred when you want glucocorticoid without mineralocorticoid side effects (cerebral edema). Hydrocortisone (= cortisol) has some MR activity — physiologic replacement. Potency: dex >> methylprednisolone > prednisone > hydrocortisone
Synthetic MINERALOCORTICOID (high MR activity) + some GR activity. Used in: primary adrenal insufficiency (replaces aldo), orthostatic hypotension. Fludrocortisone > aldosterone in MR potency because it resists 11β-HSD2 inactivation

Q1. A C21 molecule with a 4-ring nucleus, a ketone at C-3, a double bond between C-4 and C-5, and hydroxyl groups at C-11 (β), C-17 (α), and C-21 is most likely:
A. Cholesterol
B. Cortisol
C. Testosterone

B — Cortisol.
Cortisol is a C21 pregnane with characteristic hydroxyl groups at C-11β, C-17α, C-21, plus 3-oxo-Δ4 A-ring. Cholesterol (A) is C27. Testosterone (C) is C19 with 17β-OH. Estradiol (D) is C18 aromatic. Aldosterone (E) has a distinctive C-18 aldehyde.
Q2. Protein that shuttles cholesterol from OMM to IMM for steroidogenesis?
A. StAR
B. CYP11A1
C. HMG-CoA reductase
D. LDL receptor
E. Adrenodoxin reductase
A — StAR.
StAR shuttles cholesterol from OMM to IMM — the acute rate-limiting step, induced within minutes of ACTH via cAMP/PKA/CREB. CYP11A1 then CLEAVES cholesterol to pregnenolone on the IMM, but depends on StAR for substrate access. StAR mutation → congenital lipoid adrenal hyperplasia.
Q3. 52-year-old woman with adrenal adenoma, BP 195/110, K+ 2.8, metabolic alkalosis, elevated aldosterone, SUPPRESSED renin. Zone + signature enzyme?
A. Zona glomerulosa; aldosterone synthase (CYP11B2)
B. Zona fasciculata; CYP11B1
C. Zona reticularis; 17,20-lyase
D. Adrenal medulla; PNMT
E. Zona fasciculata; CYP21A2
A — Zona glomerulosa; aldosterone synthase.
Conn syndrome. Aldosterone synthase (CYP11B2) is uniquely expressed in zona glomerulosa — uses DOC and corticosterone as substrates, performs final hydroxylation and C18 oxidation to aldosterone. “Low renin” = PRIMARY (autonomous aldo suppresses RAAS).
Q4. 2-week-old 46,XX infant: ambiguous genitalia, dehydration, hyponatremia, hyperkalemia, elevated 17-OH-progesterone. Enzyme deficient + shunt?
A. 11β-hydroxylase; DOC shunt
B. 21-hydroxylase; 17-OH-progesterone → DHEA/androstenedione shunt
C. 17α-hydroxylase; aldosterone shunt
D. 3β-HSD; mineralocorticoid shunt
E. Aldosterone synthase; cortisol shunt
B — 21-hydroxylase deficiency (classic salt-wasting CAH).
MOST common CAH. 21-OH converts 17-OH-prog → 11-deoxycortisol AND progesterone → DOC. When blocked: cortisol ↓, aldo ↓ (hyponatremia, hyperkalemia, salt-wasting), 17-OH-progesterone accumulates → shunted via 17,20-lyase to DHEA/androstenedione → VIRILIZATION.
Q5. LONGEST half-life and MOST abundant circulating adrenal steroid?
A. Aldosterone
B. Cortisol
C. Free DHEA
D. DHEA-sulfate (DHEA-S)
E. 17-OH-progesterone
D — DHEA-sulfate.
Made in zona reticularis by SULT2A1. Sulfation extends half-life to 8-10+ hours, making DHEA-S the most abundant circulating steroid hormone in humans. Aldosterone: shortest (~20 min). Cortisol: ~70-90 min. Free DHEA: ~30 min.
Q6. Aldosterone transcribes an apical Na+ channel in collecting duct. Gene product?
A. SGLT2
B. ENaC
C. NKCC2
D. NCC
E. ROMK
B — ENaC.
Aldosterone → MR → transcription of ENaC (apical Na+), Na-K-ATPase (basolateral), ROMK (apical K+), SGK1. Amiloride blocks ENaC; spironolactone blocks MR upstream.
Q7. MR binds cortisol and aldosterone with similar affinity, but circulating cortisol is ~100-1000× higher than aldosterone. Why doesn't cortisol activate MR in kidney?
A. Cortisol cannot cross the membrane
B. 11β-HSD2 in collecting duct converts cortisol to cortisone (low MR affinity)
C. Cortisol is cleared by liver before reaching kidney
D. MR is only on apical membranes
E. CBG prevents cortisol entry
B — 11β-HSD2.
Kidney collecting duct cells express 11β-HSD2, which oxidizes cortisol's 11β-OH → 11-ketone → cortisone (low MR affinity). Licorice (glycyrrhetinic acid) inhibits → cortisol activates MR → “apparent mineralocorticoid excess”. Congenital 11β-HSD2 deficiency = same phenotype.
Q8. 28-year-old HIV/AIDS patient with fatigue, low cortisol, elevated ACTH, hyperpigmentation of palmar creases and buccal mucosa. Mechanism?
A. Aldosterone stimulates melanocytes
B. Low cortisol releases POMC transcription → ACTH + α-MSH rise together; MSH activates melanocortin receptors
C. 17-OH-progesterone stimulates melanocytes
D. Dexamethasone suppresses melanogenesis
E. 11β-HSD2 deficiency causes pigment retention
B — POMC produces both ACTH and α-MSH.
Primary adrenal insufficiency (Addison, here HIV-related) → low cortisol → loss of feedback → POMC transcription soars. POMC cleaves to ACTH + β-endorphin + α-MSH. Elevated α-MSH (and ACTH itself) activates MC1R on melanocytes → hyperpigmentation (palmar creases, buccal mucosa, scars). Secondary AI (pituitary) has LOW ACTH → no hyperpigmentation.
Q9. Eplerenone vs spironolactone — which is TRUE of eplerenone?
A. Eplerenone has HIGHER androgen-receptor activity → more gynecomastia
B. Eplerenone is MORE selective for MR, with less PR/AR activity → fewer endocrine side effects
C. Eplerenone blocks only ENaC
D. Eplerenone is a GR antagonist
E. Eplerenone has no effect on K+
B — Eplerenone is more MR-selective.
Spironolactone is a promiscuous steroid-receptor antagonist — MR block + anti-androgen + PR effects → gynecomastia, menstrual irregularities, decreased libido. Eplerenone is more MR-selective → fewer sex-hormone side effects but still K+-sparing. Both used in hyperaldo, heart failure, and resistant HTN. Finerenone is newer nonsteroidal MR antagonist for diabetic CKD.
Q10. FIRST committed step of steroid biosynthesis, catalyzed on the inner mitochondrial membrane?
A. Cholesterol transport into mitochondria by StAR
B. Cholesterol → pregnenolone by CYP11A1
C. 17-hydroxylation of pregnenolone by CYP17
D. 21-hydroxylation of 17-OH-progesterone by CYP21A2
E. 11β-hydroxylation of 11-deoxycortisol by CYP11B1
B — CYP11A1 (cholesterol → pregnenolone).
CYP11A1 (= P450scc = desmolase) catalyzes cleavage of the cholesterol side chain on the INNER mitochondrial membrane → pregnenolone. This is the first committed step — the first enzymatic reaction irreversibly dedicated to the steroid pathway. StAR (A) is the RATE-LIMITING shuttle (transport, not enzymatic). CYP17, CYP21, CYP11B1 are downstream pathway-specific enzymes.
