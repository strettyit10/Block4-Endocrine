Structural signatures (LO 1)
AROMATIC (benzene) A-ring with 3-OH phenol. No other steroid has this — reflects loss of C-19 methyl + aromatization
Removing C-19 angular methyl + introducing 3 double bonds in A-ring → fully aromatic benzene ring. 3-oxo converted to 3-OH phenol. Catalyzed by aromatase (CYP19)
Pregnenolone (and Δ4 form progesterone) — enters mineralocorticoid, glucocorticoid, and sex steroid pathways
Blocks androgen + estrogen synthesis (estrogens need C19 androgen substrate). Corticoids preserved (progesterone pathway intact). Sexual infantilism without virilization
Fetal androgens not converted to estrogens → virilization in utero (ambiguous genitalia at birth); postnatally: tall stature (no estrogen to close epiphyses), primary amenorrhea, polycystic ovaries, osteopenia

Two-cell / two-gonadotropin model (LO 3)
LH binds Gs-coupled receptor → cAMP/PKA → stimulates thecal steroidogenic enzymes → produces ANDROSTENEDIONE
FSH binds Gs-coupled receptor → cAMP/PKA → stimulates AROMATASE → converts thecal androstenedione → estrone → (17β-HSD1) → estradiol
Thecal cells LACK aromatase — can't convert androgens to estrogens. Androstenedione must diffuse into granulosa for estrogen production

Sustained high E2 (>200 pg/mL for ~48 h) flips feedback from negative to POSITIVE → LH SURGE → ovulation. Corpus luteum then makes progesterone (luteal phase)

Placenta and lifetime shifts (LO 3)
Placenta LACKS 17α-hydroxylase and 17,20-lyase → can't make DHEA from pregnenolone. Uses FETAL adrenal DHEA-S as substrate for estriol synthesis
E3 requires intact fetal adrenal (DHEA-S source), placenta (desulfation + aromatization), fetal-placental circulation. FALLING E3 late pregnancy signals fetal distress (demise, IUGR, placental insufficiency)

(1) Ovarian thecal overproduction (↑ LH:FSH + hyperinsulinemia stim). (2) Hyperinsulinemia SUPPRESSES hepatic SHBG synthesis → ↑ FREE testosterone. Both drive hirsutism, acne, oligomenorrhea

Inactivation and clearance (LO 5)
Glucuronide and sulfate conjugation at hydroxyl groups → water-soluble → kidney excretion. Also oxidation/reduction reactions. Liver is major clearance organ
17β-HSD2 (OXIDATIVE, opposite of HSD1) converts estradiol → estrone. 16α-hydroxylation (liver) converts estrone → estriol. Both weaker than E2 but can still bind ER
Extensive FIRST-PASS hepatic metabolism → very little reaches circulation. Low bioavailability + hepatotoxicity risk with oral 17α-alkylated variants
IM (cypionate, enanthate — long-acting depot), transdermal (patch, gel), implantable pellets, intranasal. Avoid oral 17α-alkylated forms (hepatotoxic)
(1) Reduced hepatic clearance → estrogen accumulates. (2) ↑ SHBG from systemic estrogen. (3) Impaired conjugation. (4) Hypoalbuminemia alters kinetics. Result: gynecomastia, spider angiomas, testicular atrophy, ↓ libido
LO 6
ON EXAM — AR/ER/PR are Type I nuclear receptors: cytoplasmic + HSP90 at rest → ligand → HSP release → dimer → nucleus → HRE. ERα (ESR1): breast/uterus/bone/liver. ERβ (ESR2): prostate/ovary/CNS/vascular. Membrane receptors (GPER, mPR) for rapid non-genomic effects. SERMs (tamoxifen, raloxifene, clomiphene), AIs, anti-androgens, 5α-red inhibitors, mifepristone
Canonical Type I nuclear receptor mechanism (LO 6)
StepWhat happens
1. Resting
AR, ER, PR sit in cytoplasm bound to HSP90 + HSP70 chaperones (Type I NR mechanism, same as MR, GR)
2. Ligand entry
Lipophilic hormone crosses plasma membrane by diffusion (no transporter)
3. Chaperone release
Ligand binds LBD → conformational change → HSP chaperones dissociate
4. Dimerization
Receptor dimerizes (AR homodimer; ER α-α, β-β, or α-β heterodimer; PR homodimer) → translocates to nucleus
5. DNA binding
Dimer binds palindromic HRE (ARE/ERE/PRE) on target gene promoters; recruits coactivators (agonist) or corepressors (antagonist) → modulates transcription
6. Genomic timescale
Hours — requires new protein synthesis. Full effect over hours to days
7. Non-genomic
Membrane receptors (GPER for estrogen; mPR for progesterone) trigger rapid (sec-min) MAPK, PI3K/AKT, cAMP signaling. Ex: estrogen vasodilation, CNS mood effects, cardioprotection
ERα vs ERβ (LO 6)
Dominant in BREAST, UTERUS, LIVER, BONE, hypothalamus. Drives classic estrogenic effects — breast proliferation, endometrial growth, bone preservation, hepatic clotting factor + binding protein synthesis. Main SERM target
Dominant in PROSTATE, OVARY, CNS, cardiovascular, lung, colon. Often opposes / modulates ERα. Newer selective ERβ agonists under investigation
Different tissues have distinct ERα/ERβ ratios + distinct coactivator/corepressor availability + distinct chromatin contexts. SERM-bound receptor adopts a conformation favoring coactivators in some tissues (agonist) and corepressors in others (antagonist)

Clinical syndromes (LO 6)
Complete Androgen Insensitivity Syndrome — AR loss-of-function mutation (X chromosome). X-LINKED RECESSIVE. 46,XY with functional Sertoli + Leydig cells but nonfunctional AR
Leydig cells make testosterone normally. Peripheral AROMATASE converts T → 17β-estradiol → ERα drives breast development. Uterus + upper vagina absent (MIS from Sertoli cells caused Müllerian regression)
Pubic + axillary hair are AR-dependent (driven by DHT). Nonfunctional AR → fail to develop despite high T/DHT. Scalp hair preserved (estrogen-dependent). Classic CAIS finding
BIRTH: 46,XY ambiguous / female-appearing external genitalia (no DHT for external virilization); Wolffian preserved internally. PUBERTY: rising testosterone overcomes DHT block → virilization ("guevedoces")
(1) Increased ENDOMETRIAL CANCER risk (agonist in endometrium) — always investigate postmenopausal bleeding. (2) VTE (DVT, PE). Also hot flashes, cataracts, hepatic steatosis

Q4. Two-cell / two-gonadotropin model — correct pairing?
A. Theca — FSH — aromatase → E2
B. Granulosa — LH — androstenedione
C. Theca — LH — androstenedione; granulosa — FSH — aromatase → estrone → (17β-HSD1) → E2
D. Theca — FSH — progesterone; granulosa — LH — testosterone

C — LH theca/androstenedione; FSH granulosa/aromatase.
Thecal cells lack aromatase — can't make estrogens themselves — but LH (Gs-cAMP) stimulates their androgen output. Androstenedione diffuses into granulosa, where FSH-driven aromatase → estrone, and 17β-HSD1 → 17β-estradiol. Foundation of menstrual cycle physiology.
Q5. 58-year-old postmenopausal woman with ER+ breast CA. Dominant estrogen source?
A. Ovarian granulosa (remaining follicles)
B. Adrenal cortex directly
C. Peripheral aromatization of adrenal ANDROSTENEDIONE to ESTRONE in adipose
D. Placental estriol
E. Liver synthesis
C — Peripheral aromatization of androstenedione to estrone in adipose.
After menopause, ovaries depleted and granulosa E2 stops. Dominant postmenopausal estrogen = estrone, produced when adrenal androstenedione is aromatized by peripheral aromatase in adipose (+ bone, skin, brain). Obesity RAISES postmenopausal estrone → endometrial hyperplasia/CA risk. Why aromatase inhibitors are first-line for postmenopausal ER+ breast CA.
Q6. 28-year-old PCOS patient: hirsutism, oligomenorrhea, acne, BMI 33. Free T elevated; total T at upper limit of normal. Mechanism for elevated free T?
A. Adrenal tumor
B. HYPERINSULINEMIA and obesity suppress hepatic SHBG → raise free T
C. Hepatic failure with markedly ↑ SHBG
D. 17α-OH CAH
E. Exogenous T
B — Hyperinsulinemia + obesity LOWER SHBG.
PCOS hyperandrogenism has TWO mechanisms: (1) ovarian thecal overproduction (LH-driven), (2) hyperinsulinemia + obesity suppress hepatic SHBG → more free T for the same total. Metformin and weight loss raise SHBG. Converse: pregnancy/OCPs/hyperthyroid RAISE SHBG.
Q7. Postmenopausal woman on tamoxifen for adjuvant ER+ breast CA — expected effect?
A. Agonist in breast (blocks tumor)
B. Antagonist in breast (blocks tumor) + AGONIST in endometrium (↑ endometrial CA risk) + agonist in bone (maintains density)
C. Antagonist in all tissues
D. Aromatase inhibitor
E. PR antagonist
B — Tamoxifen: antagonist breast; agonist endometrium + bone.
SERM tissue selectivity from differential coactivator/corepressor recruitment + ERα/ERβ ratios. Tamoxifen BLOCKS breast ER (tumor suppression) but STIMULATES endometrium (↑ endometrial hyperplasia/CA risk) + bone (maintains density). Also ↑ VTE. Raloxifene: antagonist in breast AND endometrium but agonist in bone — osteoporosis + breast CA prevention without endometrial risk.
Q8. 35-year-old with anovulatory PCOS wants to conceive. Clomiphene mechanism?
A. Direct ovarian follicle stimulation
B. ER agonist at hypothalamus
C. ER ANTAGONIST at hypothalamus → removes negative feedback → ↑ GnRH pulsatility → ↑ LH/FSH → ovulation
D. PR agonist
E. Aromatase inhibitor
C — Clomiphene blocks hypothalamic ER.
Clomiphene blocks estrogen feedback at hypothalamic ER → hypothalamus “thinks” estrogen is low → ↑ GnRH pulse frequency/amplitude → ↑ LH/FSH from pituitary → follicle recruitment + ovulation. First-line for ovulatory induction in PCOS. Letrozole (AI) now also used off-label.
Q9. Pregnant woman with markedly elevated TOTAL testosterone but normal FREE T and no hyperandrogenism signs. Explanation?
A. Luteoma of pregnancy
B. Estrogen-driven ↑ hepatic SHBG synthesis raises total but not free testosterone
C. Placental aromatase failure
D. PCOS relapse
E. Adrenal androgen tumor
B — Pregnancy estrogen ↑ SHBG.
Pregnancy dramatically raises estrogen → hepatic SHBG synthesis up. Total T rises (more bound) but biologically active FREE T stays normal (HPG axis defends it). Same principle: CBG (total cortisol up, free normal) and TBG (total T4 up, free normal) — all driven by estrogen-hepatic binding-globulin effect. Don't misinterpret elevated total as hyperandrogen state.
Q10. 32-year-old man on flutamide for prostate CA. Mechanism?
A. 5α-reductase inhibitor
B. Aromatase inhibitor
C. AR antagonist — blocks androgen at the receptor
D. GnRH agonist
E. PR antagonist
C — Flutamide is an AR antagonist.
Flutamide, bicalutamide, enzalutamide are nonsteroidal AR antagonists for prostate CA — often combined with GnRH agonist leuprolide for COMBINED ANDROGEN BLOCKADE. Bind AR and prevent T/DHT from activating it. SE: gynecomastia (unopposed estradiol signaling), hepatotoxicity (especially flutamide). Compare: 5α-reductase inhibitors (finasteride) block DHT synthesis; AIs block estrogen synthesis; leuprolide continuous desensitizes GnRH receptors.
