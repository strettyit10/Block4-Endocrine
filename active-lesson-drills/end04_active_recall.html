LO 1
ON EXAM — ALL steroids share the cyclopentanoperhydrophenanthrene 4-ring nucleus (rings A, B, C + 5-member D). Cholesterol = C27. Classes by carbon number: C21 PREGNANES (progestins, gluco, mineralo); C19 ANDROSTANES (androgens); C18 ESTRANES (estrogens, aromatic A ring). α = below plane, β = above plane. Key suffixes: -one = keto, -ol = hydroxyl, Δ or ene = double bond. Cortisol = 11β-OH, 17α-OH, 21-OH, 3-oxo-Δ4. Aldosterone = distinctive 18-aldehyde

LO 2
ON EXAM — ALL steroidogenesis shares: (1) cholesterol sourcing (LDL/SR-BI/de novo/lipid droplets mobilized by HSL under PKA); (2) StAR shuttles cholesterol OMM → IMM (ACUTE regulated rate-limiting step); (3) CYP11A1 (P450scc/desmolase) cleaves cholesterol → PREGNENOLONE on IMM (first committed step); (4) adrenodoxin reductase → adrenodoxin → CYP11A1 electron shuttle; (5) pregnenolone exits mitochondria for further modification in smooth ER (CYP17, CYP21) with some steps back in mitochondria (CYP11B1/B2); (6) 3β-HSD converts Δ5-steroids (pregnenolone, DHEA, 17-OH-pregnenolone) to Δ4-steroids (progesterone, androstenedione, 17-OH-progesterone)

LO 3
ON EXAM — 3 CORTICAL zones (outer → inner: GFR): Glomerulosa (salt — aldosterone only), Fasciculata (sugar — cortisol), Reticularis (sex — DHEA). PLUS adrenal medulla (neural crest → epinephrine). Regulation: Ang II + K+ drive glomerulosa; ACTH drives fasciculata + reticularis (NOT glomerulosa). Enzyme ownership: aldosterone synthase (CYP11B2) ONLY in glomerulosa; 17α-hydroxylase (CYP17) NOT in glomerulosa → explains why it can't make cortisol or androgens

LO 4
ON EXAM — All 3 pathways start with CHOLESTEROL → (StAR + CYP11A1) → PREGNENOLONE. Aldosterone: pregnenolone → progesterone (3β-HSD) → DOC (CYP21) → corticosterone (CYP11B2) → aldosterone (CYP11B2). Cortisol: pregnenolone → 17-OH-pregnenolone (CYP17) → 17-OH-progesterone (3β-HSD) → 11-deoxycortisol (CYP21) → cortisol (CYP11B1). DHEA: 17-OH-pregnenolone → DHEA (CYP17, 17,20-lyase) → androstenedione (3β-HSD) → testosterone (17β-HSD)

LO 5
ON EXAM — Aldosterone: loose albumin binding (~60%), very short half-life (~20 min), inactivated by liver → tetrahydroaldosterone-glucuronide. Cortisol: CBG carries ~80% high-affinity + albumin ~15%, half-life ~70-90 min, inactivated by liver (reduction + glucuronidation) AND kidney 11β-HSD2 (cortisol → cortisone). DHEA: weak albumin binding; most circulates as DHEA-S (sulfated in zona reticularis by SULT2A1) — LONGEST half-life of any steroid (8-10 hours), most abundant circulating steroid. Pregnancy ↑ CBG → total cortisol ↑ but free normal. Licorice (glycyrrhetinic acid) inhibits 11β-HSD2 → APPARENT MINERALOCORTICOID EXCESS

LO 6
ON EXAM — ALDOSTERONE on MR (cytoplasmic) in principal cells of collecting duct: dimer → nucleus → transcribes ENaC (apical), Na-K-ATPase (basolateral), ROMK (apical K+ exit), SGK1. Result: Na+/H2O retention + K+ and H+ secretion. CORTISOL on GR (widely expressed): trans-activation (PEPCK, G6Pase, annexin-1, IκB) + trans-repression (blocks NF-κB, AP-1 → anti-inflammatory). Rapid NON-genomic effects also exist. 11β-HSD2 converts cortisol to cortisone in kidney → protects MR from cortisol (which binds MR as well as aldo does). Spironolactone/eplerenone/finerenone = MR antagonists

10 questions — weighted across LOs with emphasis on synthesis steps and mechanisms.
Q1. An investigator synthesizes a novel steroid and characterizes its structure as a C21 molecule with a 4-ring nucleus, a ketone at C-3, a double bond between C-4 and C-5, and hydroxyl groups at C-11 (β), C-17 (α), and C-21. The molecule is MOST likely which of the following?
A. Cholesterol
B. Cortisol
C. Testosterone
D. 17β-Estradiol
E. Aldosterone
B — Cortisol.
Cortisol is a C21 pregnane with characteristic hydroxyl groups at C-11β, C-17α, and C-21, plus a 3-oxo-Δ4 A-ring (ketone at C-3 with double bond C4-C5). Cholesterol (A) is C27. Testosterone (C) is C19 with only an 11β-OH absent and 17β-OH. Estradiol (D) is C18 with an aromatic A-ring. Aldosterone (E) has a distinctive C-18 aldehyde (hence the name).
Q2. In response to a surge of ACTH, an adrenocortical cell must rapidly deliver cholesterol from its outer mitochondrial membrane to its inner mitochondrial membrane so that steroidogenesis can begin. The protein responsible is:
A. StAR (Steroidogenic Acute Regulatory protein)
B. CYP11A1 (P450scc / desmolase)
C. HMG-CoA reductase
D. LDL receptor
E. Adrenodoxin reductase
A — StAR.
StAR shuttles cholesterol from outer to inner mitochondrial membrane — this is the acute rate-limiting step of steroidogenesis, induced within minutes of ACTH via cAMP/PKA/CREB. CYP11A1 (B) actually CLEAVES cholesterol to pregnenolone on the IMM, but substrate access depends on StAR. Without StAR, no steroids form → congenital lipoid adrenal hyperplasia.
Q3. A 52-year-old woman with a unilateral adrenal adenoma presents with resistant HTN (195/110), serum K+ 2.8 mEq/L, and metabolic alkalosis. Plasma aldosterone is markedly elevated; plasma renin is SUPPRESSED. The excess hormone is made in which adrenal zone and by which signature enzyme?
A. Zona glomerulosa; aldosterone synthase (CYP11B2)
B. Zona fasciculata; 11β-hydroxylase (CYP11B1)
C. Zona reticularis; 17,20-lyase (CYP17)
D. Adrenal medulla; phenylethanolamine N-methyltransferase
E. Zona fasciculata; 21-hydroxylase (CYP21A2)
A — Zona glomerulosa; aldosterone synthase.
Conn syndrome (primary hyperaldosteronism) from an adrenal adenoma. Aldosterone synthase (CYP11B2) is uniquely expressed in zona glomerulosa — it uses DOC and corticosterone as substrates and performs the final hydroxylation and C18 oxidation to yield aldosterone. CYP11B1 (B) in fasciculata makes cortisol (different terminal enzyme). The clue “low renin” = PRIMARY (autonomous aldosterone suppresses RAAS).
Q4. A 2-week-old 46,XX infant presents with ambiguous genitalia, dehydration, vomiting, hyponatremia, hyperkalemia, and elevated serum 17-OH-progesterone. Which enzyme is deficient and which precursor is shunted to cause virilization?
A. 11β-hydroxylase; shunt to 11-deoxycorticosterone
B. 21-hydroxylase; shunt from 17-OH-progesterone into the androgen pathway (DHEA, androstenedione)
C. 17α-hydroxylase; shunt to aldosterone
D. 3β-HSD; shunt to mineralocorticoid pathway
E. Aldosterone synthase; shunt to cortisol
B — 21-hydroxylase deficiency (classic salt-wasting CAH).
The MOST common CAH. 21-hydroxylase converts 17-OH-progesterone → 11-deoxycortisol (cortisol path) and progesterone → DOC (aldo path). When blocked: cortisol ↓, aldosterone ↓ (→ hyponatremia, hyperkalemia, salt-wasting crisis), and 17-OH-progesterone accumulates → shunted via 17,20-lyase toward DHEA/androstenedione → VIRILIZATION of 46,XX infants. Screening lab: elevated 17-OH-progesterone.
Q5. Which of the following steroid hormones has the LONGEST plasma half-life and is the MOST abundant circulating adrenal steroid?
A. Aldosterone
B. Cortisol
C. Free DHEA
D. DHEA-sulfate (DHEA-S)
E. 17-OH-progesterone
D — DHEA-sulfate.
DHEA-S is made in the zona reticularis by SULT2A1 (sulfotransferase). Sulfation dramatically prolongs half-life (8–10+ hours) by slowing clearance, making DHEA-S the most abundant circulating steroid hormone in humans. Aldosterone (A) has the shortest half-life (~20 min, loose albumin binding only). Cortisol is ~70–90 min. Free DHEA is short (~30 min); sulfation is the trick that extends it.
Q6. In principal cells of the cortical collecting duct, aldosterone binding to the mineralocorticoid receptor increases transcription of a Na+ channel in the apical membrane. The gene product is:
A. SGLT2 (sodium-glucose cotransporter 2)
B. ENaC (epithelial Na+ channel)
C. NKCC2 (Na-K-2Cl cotransporter)
D. NCC (Na-Cl cotransporter)
E. ROMK (renal outer medullary K+)
B — ENaC.
Aldosterone binds cytoplasmic MR → dimer → nucleus → transcription of ENaC (apical Na+ channel), Na-K-ATPase (basolateral), ROMK (apical K+ exit — answer E is in the cell but is distinct), and SGK1. Amiloride blocks ENaC; spironolactone blocks MR upstream. NKCC2 is in thick ascending limb; NCC is in distal convoluted tubule; SGLT2 is in proximal tubule.
Q7. The mineralocorticoid receptor (MR) binds cortisol and aldosterone with SIMILAR affinity. Because circulating cortisol concentrations are ~100–1000× higher than aldosterone, why doesn't cortisol chronically activate MR in the kidney?
A. Cortisol cannot cross the plasma membrane of collecting duct cells
B. 11β-HSD2 in collecting duct cells converts cortisol to cortisone, which does NOT bind MR
C. Cortisol is selectively cleared by the liver before reaching the kidney
D. MR is expressed only on apical membranes, where aldosterone uniquely acts
E. CBG prevents cortisol from entering cells
B — 11β-HSD2.
Kidney collecting duct cells express 11β-HSD2, which oxidizes cortisol's 11β-OH to an 11-ketone, producing cortisone. Cortisone has low affinity for MR, so cortisol doesn't activate MR. Licorice (glycyrrhetinic acid) inhibits 11β-HSD2 → cortisol activates MR → “apparent mineralocorticoid excess” (HTN + hypokalemia + low aldo + low renin). Congenital 11β-HSD2 deficiency = same phenotype.
Q8. A 28-year-old man with HIV/AIDS is evaluated for persistent fatigue. Morning cortisol is low-normal; ACTH is markedly elevated; skin hyperpigmentation is noted on his buccal mucosa and palmar creases. Which BEST explains the hyperpigmentation?
A. Excess aldosterone stimulates melanocytes
B. Low cortisol releases pituitary POMC transcription → ACTH and α-MSH rise together (MSH is a POMC cleavage product that activates melanocortin receptors)
C. Accumulated 17-OH-progesterone is a melanocyte stimulant
D. Dexamethasone suppresses melanogenesis
E. 11β-HSD2 deficiency causes pigment retention
B — POMC cleavage produces both ACTH and α-MSH.
Primary adrenal insufficiency (Addison disease, here from HIV → adrenal infection/destruction) → low cortisol → loss of negative feedback → pituitary POMC transcription soars. POMC is cleaved into ACTH + β-endorphin + α-MSH. Elevated α-MSH (and ACTH itself, which also has some melanocortin activity) activates MC1R on melanocytes → hyperpigmentation (palmar creases, buccal mucosa, scars). Secondary adrenal insufficiency (pituitary cause) does NOT cause hyperpigmentation because ACTH/POMC are low.
Q9. A 45-year-old woman has Cushing syndrome. Her physician considers two MR antagonists: spironolactone and eplerenone. Which is TRUE of eplerenone relative to spironolactone?
A. Eplerenone has HIGHER androgen-receptor activity and is more likely to cause gynecomastia
B. Eplerenone is MORE selective for MR, with less progesterone-receptor and androgen-receptor activity → fewer endocrine side effects (less gynecomastia, less menstrual irregularity)
C. Eplerenone blocks only ENaC and spares MR
D. Eplerenone is a potent GR antagonist
E. Eplerenone has no effect on K+ balance
B — Eplerenone is more MR-selective than spironolactone.
Spironolactone is a promiscuous steroid-receptor antagonist — MR block + anti-androgen effects + progesterone-receptor effects → gynecomastia (men), menstrual irregularities (women), decreased libido. Eplerenone is more MR-selective → fewer sex-hormone side effects but still K+-sparing. Both used in hyperaldosteronism, heart failure, and resistant HTN. Finerenone is a newer nonsteroidal MR antagonist used in diabetic CKD.
Q10. Which of the following is the FIRST committed step of steroid biosynthesis (shared by all steroidogenic tissues), catalyzed in the INNER mitochondrial membrane?
A. Cholesterol transport into mitochondria by StAR
B. Conversion of cholesterol to pregnenolone by CYP11A1 (side-chain cleavage)
C. Hydroxylation of pregnenolone at C-17 by CYP17
D. 21-hydroxylation of 17-OH-progesterone by CYP21A2
E. 11β-hydroxylation of 11-deoxycortisol by CYP11B1
B — CYP11A1 (cholesterol → pregnenolone).
CYP11A1 (= P450scc = desmolase) catalyzes cleavage of the cholesterol side chain on the INNER mitochondrial membrane, producing pregnenolone. This is the first committed step of steroidogenesis — the first enzymatic reaction irreversibly dedicated to the steroid pathway. StAR (A) is the RATE-LIMITING shuttle (transport, not enzymatic conversion). CYP17 (C), CYP21 (D), CYP11B1 (E) are downstream, pathway-specific enzymes.
